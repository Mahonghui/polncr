Pan-cancer transcriptomic analysis associates long


last edit: 2017-01-03

On 25 October 2016, Arghavan Ashouri et al. published a paper online providing a comprehensive overview of long non-coding RNA (lncRNA) transcriptional alterations in relation to key driver mutational events in human cancers. They used transcriptome and exome sequencing data from thousands of tumours across 19 cancer types for the study. As a result, they confirmed that many new lncRNAs are associated to somatic mutations in key oncogenic driver genes. They highlight the lncRNAs with possible functions downstream of the tumour suppressor TP53 and the master antioxidant transcription factor NFE2L2.
